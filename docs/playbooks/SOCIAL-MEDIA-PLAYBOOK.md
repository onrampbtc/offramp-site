## 1. Brand voice, distilled

Offramp is the gold buyer that shows its work. The voice is **honest, warm, precise**:

- **Honest** — every number is exact and sourced from our own published data. We say what we don't know ("provisional," "range," "final price comes from a recorded assay"). We never manufacture urgency or proof.
- **Warm** — we talk to the person with the dusty drawer and the inherited jewelry box, not to "sellers." Plain words. No finance-bro swagger, no pawn-counter hustle.
- **Precise** — numbers are exact (58.3%, not "about 60%"), dated where relevant, and cited. If a number would go stale (spot price), we link the live tool instead of quoting it.

### Hard copy laws (from BUILD-SPEC — violations fail review)
1. **Never write:** "unlock the value", "turn clutter into cash", countdown timers or any urgency mechanic, or "XRF" (say "an assay you can watch" / "assayed on camera").
2. **"Cash out" only at decision moments** (offer accept, vault sell, alerts) — never in first-contact copy, which social always is. So: never on social.
3. **No fabricated proof.** No invented testimonials, no fake payout screenshots, no "Sarah from Austin got $1,240." Real payouts only once they exist and are published on the site.
4. **Every stat cited to our own published data** (the 150-buyer study, the question bank, karat math on our calculator pages). Post format: state the number + where it comes from.
5. **Never quote a spot price in a post.** It goes stale the hour it publishes. Reference the fact that gold moved / where it is, and link `offrampgold.com/gold-calculator` or `/gold-price` for the live number.
6. **BTC is a differentiator, not acquisition.** Bitcoin payout may appear as a closing detail ("wire, or bitcoin via Onramp"), never as the hook of a post.

### Five good/bad pairs

| ✗ Bad (why it fails) | ✓ Good |
|---|---|
| "Turn that drawer of clutter into CASH today! 💰" (banned phrase, urgency, hype) | "That drawer you never open? Photograph what's in it and we'll tell you what it's actually worth — in about a minute. offrampgold.com/ask" |
| "Gold just hit $3,412/oz — sell NOW before it drops!" (stale number, urgency, market timing advice) | "Gold moved again this week. Whatever today's number is, your 14k piece is worth 58.3% of it by weight. See the live math: offrampgold.com/gold-calculator" |
| "Our customers LOVE us — 5 stars everywhere! ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐" (fabricated proof) | "We reviewed 150 local gold buyers across 20 metros. 138 of them publish no payout rate at all. We think you should see the math before you walk in anywhere — including here." |
| "We use state-of-the-art XRF technology for precision testing!" (banned term, jargon-as-flex) | "Every final price comes from an assay you can watch — recorded, explained, and done before you decide anything." |
| "Don't get SCAMMED by pawn shops!! 😱 They're ripping you off!" (fear-bait, attacks named competitors) | "A quote 'per pennyweight' sounds bigger than a quote per gram — 1 dwt is 1.555 grams. Same gold, different unit. Always ask for the per-gram number." |

The pattern: the bad versions sell feelings; the good versions hand the reader a fact they can verify and a tool they can use. **We are the buyer that does the reader's homework for them.**

## 2. Platform strategy

### X (@offrampgold) — market context, honesty stats, Ask Offramp demos
- The wonk channel. Short, numerate, screenshot-friendly.
- Post types: single stats from the 150-buyer study, karat math, "what people actually search" data, quick Ask Offramp demo clips/screenshots, spot-move commentary (no numbers — link the calculator).
- Tone: dry, confident, zero emoji or at most one. Numbers do the flexing.
- Cadence: 7/week. Limit 280 chars.

### Facebook / Instagram (@offrampgold) — inheritor warmth, education carousels
- The channel for the actual seller demographic: people who inherited a jewelry box, are downsizing, or finally opened the drawer. (Worthy photographs 50-somethings at home for a reason — that's who sells.)
- Post types: education carousels (copy + image brief per slide), warm invitations, "how to read the stamp inside the ring" explainers, the recorded-assay promise.
- Tone: warm, unhurried, respectful of the object's history. Never "clutter." These pieces were someone's.
- Every post ships as **copy + image brief** (the brief describes the visual for later production — bone/paper background, ink text, gold only as photographed material, documentary captions, no shimmer/glow).
- Cadence: 3/week, cross-posted FB+IG with format tweaks.

### LinkedIn (company page + Michael's founder posts) — the transparency-industry angle
- The founder-POV channel. The story is: *an industry where 92% of buyers won't publish a rate, and what it looks like to build the opposite.*
- Post types: the 150-buyer study findings as industry analysis, why-we-built-it essays (Ask Offramp, the recorded assay, "provisional" labels as product architecture), honest build-in-public notes.
- Tone: first person, direct, specific numbers, no thought-leader throat-clearing.
- Cadence: 1–2/week.

## 4. Rules (enforced at draft review)

1. Every stat cited to our own published data — name the source in-post or in-thread ("we reviewed 150 buyer websites across 20 metros").
2. No engagement-bait: no "RT if…", no rage-hooks, no fake polls, no "most people don't know this 🧵" throat-clearing.
3. No fabricated testimonials or payouts. Real payout posts begin only when real published payouts exist (site rule: ticker hidden until ≥5 real payouts — same threshold here).
4. No urgency mechanics: no countdowns, "today only", "before it's too late", or price-drop fear.
5. No spot-price quotes in post text. Link `/gold-calculator` or `/gold-price`.
6. No attacks on named competitors. We publish category-level data ("138 of 150"), never "Buyer X is hiding their rates."
7. Copy laws from §1 apply verbatim (no "unlock the value", "turn clutter into cash", "XRF", first-contact "cash out").
8. Numbers exact, in the site's spirit (tabular, dated where relevant). "58.3%", not "nearly 60%".
9. Links point to offrampgold.com pages that exist. Check before queueing.
10. If a post cites the 150-buyer study before a public methodology/directory page exists, phrase as "we reviewed 150 local buyer websites across 20 metros (July 2026)" — first-party research, dated. Once the study page ships, link it.

Queues live in `docs/social/queue/`, one file per fortnight, named by ISO week of the first Monday: `2026-W29.md`. Each entry:

```markdown
### [platform] · [suggested day] · [pillar]
**Status:** draft | approved | posted (YYYY-MM-DD)
**Text:**
> The post text, ready to copy-paste, within platform limits
> (X ≤ 280 chars incl. a 23-char t.co link; FB/IG ~150 words; LinkedIn ~1,300 chars).

**Link:** https://offrampgold.com/...
**Image brief:** (visual posts only) one paragraph describing the asset —
palette (bone/paper/ink, hunter/ember accents), layout, exact text on the
image, photographic direction if any. Gold appears only as photographed
material. No shimmer/glow/pulse.
```

Status flow: agent writes `draft` → Michael flips to `approved` (edits welcome) → whoever posts flips to `posted (date)`. Nothing posts from `draft`.

## 8. Automation hooks — exact credentials needed per platform

**Prerequisite for everything:** register the **@offrampgold** handle on X, Instagram, a Facebook Page, and a LinkedIn Company Page (Michael's checklist in STATE.md — still open as of 2026-07-11). Use michael@offrampgold.com as the account email.

### X (Twitter) — API v2 `POST /2/tweets`
- X Developer account + App on the **Basic paid tier** (free tier: read-only-ish, too limited for reliable posting).
- Credentials to store: `X_API_KEY`, `X_API_KEY_SECRET`, `X_ACCESS_TOKEN`, `X_ACCESS_TOKEN_SECRET` (OAuth 1.0a user context) — or OAuth 2.0 `X_CLIENT_ID` / `X_CLIENT_SECRET` + refresh token with `tweet.read tweet.write users.read offline.access` scopes.
- App permissions set to **Read and Write**.

### Facebook + Instagram — Meta Graph API
- Facebook Page (@offrampgold) + Instagram **Professional** account linked to that Page via Meta Business Suite.
- Meta developer app (Business type), Business verification for production access.
- Credentials to store: `META_PAGE_ID`, `META_IG_USER_ID`, `META_PAGE_ACCESS_TOKEN` (long-lived, 60-day — needs refresh routine).
- Permissions: `pages_manage_posts`, `pages_read_engagement`, `instagram_basic`, `instagram_content_publish`.
- Note: IG publishing via API requires an image/video (`/media` → `/media_publish`); FB Page accepts text-only (`/feed`).

### LinkedIn — Posts API
- LinkedIn Company Page + developer app approved for the **Community Management API** (posting as an organization requires this product access).
- Credentials to store: `LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID`, `LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET`, OAuth refresh token with `w_organization_social` scope, and `LINKEDIN_ORG_URN` (`urn:li:organization:{id}`).
- Founder-POV posts from Michael's personal profile: `w_member_social` scope on his token (or Michael posts those manually — recommended; personal posts read better unautomated).

### Shortcut option — Buffer (or similar scheduler)
- One `BUFFER_ACCESS_TOKEN` covers X + FB + IG + LinkedIn once the channels are connected in Buffer's UI. Fastest path to automation; trade-off is a third-party dependency and per-channel plan cost.

All tokens live in `.env.local` + Vercel env per spec §13 — never in this repo or docs.
